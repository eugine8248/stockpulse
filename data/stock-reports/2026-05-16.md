## Executive Summary
The narrative shifted sharply on May 15: the 10-year Treasury yield spiked to 4.595% and the 30-year hit 5.127% (highest since 2007), triggering a broad tech selloff that took the S&P 500 down 1.24% to 7,408 and Nasdaq down 1.54% to 26,225 — undoing two days of record highs. NVDA fell 4.4% after Trump confirmed Chinese firms have declined to buy H200 chips ("they want to develop their own"), neutralizing the May 14 export-approval catalyst just five days before May 20 earnings; the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index dropped 4%. The top risk has now flipped from "hawkish Fed pivot" to **actively realised**: BofA has pushed the next Fed cut into 2027, June FOMC cut probability has collapsed to ~28%, and the MSCI Malaysia rebalance (Axiata, YTL, Petronas Dagangan, Nestle Malaysia removed; effective close May 29) compounds passive-outflow pressure on KLSE heavyweights — we trim NVDA, GOOGL, MSFT scores, hold AVGO/META/AMZN ranks, and downgrade 1295.KL from WATCH to HOLD pending the rebalance settling.
